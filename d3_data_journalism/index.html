Correlations Discovered Between Age, Poverty and Healthcare Access

From this analysis we can see that Mississippi (MS- 21.5%) has the highest percent of their population in poverty. They are closely followed by New Mexico (NM- 21.3%). While these populations display higher than average poverty, they do preform at or near the average for Percent of Population with Access to Healthcare. Thus, the population in poverty can benefit from healthcare services. Texas (TX) has the highest Percent of Population with Access to Healthcare and a percent of poverty that is slightly higher than average.

States like Massaachusettes (MA) and Vermont (VT) display lower than average Percent of Population in Poverty, with 11.6% and 12.2% of their populations in poverty. However, they also indicate having the lowest values for % of Population with Access to Healthcare.

The Average Age of most states clusters between 35 years old and 40 years of age. Again, Massachusettes (MA) and Vermont (VT) display lowest access to health care for their population. They have an average age of 39 and 43. Utah (UT) has the youngest population and slightly above average access to health care. Maine (ME) displays the oldest average age, at 44 years old, with average access to healthcare services
